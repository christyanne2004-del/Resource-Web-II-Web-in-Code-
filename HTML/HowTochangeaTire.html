How to change a flat tire nine easy steps


Knowing how to change a flat tire is an essential driving skill. Flat tires can happen anywhere, and being prepared can save you time, money, and stress. With the right tools and a few simple steps, you can safely replace a flat tire and get back on the road quickly.



1. Park in a safe location

First things first, find a safe haven. Look for flat, firm ground away from traffic. Avoid soft dirt, grassy areas, or blind spots. Once parked, engage the parking brake and turn on your hazard lights so other drivers know you're there.


2. Gather tools and supplies

Make sure you have the essentials: a car jack, lug wrench, and a spare tire. Your owner's manual is also helpful because it contains instructions specific to your vehicle. Extra items like wheel wedges, a pressure gauge, or a flathead screwdriver can also make the job easier and safer.


3. Loosen the lug nuts

Before lifting the car, loosen the lug nuts slightly using your lug wrench. Do not remove them yet. It's easier while the tire is still touching the ground because the wheel won’t spin. If the nuts are hidden behind a hubcap, remove it carefully with a flathead screwdriver.

4. Position the jack and raise the car

Check your owner's manual for the correct jack points. Place the jack there and slowly raise the car until the flat tire is off the ground and you have enough space to install the spare.

5. Remove the lug nuts and flat tire

Finish removing the loosened lug nuts and keep them in a safe place. Then pull the flat tire straight toward you until it comes off the wheel studs.


6. Install the spare tire

Align the spare tire with the wheel studs and slide it into place. Screw the lug nuts back on by hand, but don't tighten them fully yet while the wheel is still in the air.

7. Lower the vehicle

Slowly lower the car until the tire touches the ground, then remove the jack. Tighten the lug nuts fully in a crisscross pattern using the lug wrench.

8. Tighten the lug nuts

Using your lug wrench, tighten the lug nuts one last time in a crisscross pattern or according to your owner's manual. According to Popular Mechanics, failing to tighten them completely could result in a dangerous situation.

9. Put away your tools and check tire pressure

Once the spare tire is mounted, collect your tools and put them somewhere secure. You never know when you'll need them again. Next, ensure all of your tires have the proper amount of tire pressure. You can visit a gas station with an air compressor if needed, or use a portable one to inflate the tire manually.
