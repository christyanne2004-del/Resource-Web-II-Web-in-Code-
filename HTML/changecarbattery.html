Step by step guide on : How to change your



1. Safety First

Park your car on a flat, dry surface before you begin. Be sure the vehicle is in Park and the emergency brake is engaged. Turn off the ignition and allow the car to cool. It's recommended to wear safety gloves and glasses, and never smoke under the hood.

2. Find the Battery

Carefully open the hood to locate the battery. It is usually installed in one of the front corners of the engine compartment.

3. Find & Disconnect the Terminals

Locate the positive (+) and negative (–) terminals. The positive is usually red, and the negative is black. Remove coverings if needed. Disconnect the negative cable first, then the positive, using a wrench or terminal puller.

4. Remove the Battery

Batteries are often secured with a bracket or strap. Remove or loosen it, then carefully lift the battery out and place it on a stable surface. Ask for help if needed.

5. Clean Battery Terminals

Clean any corrosion from the terminals using a terminal cleaning tool before installing the new battery.

6. Install the New Battery

Place the new battery in position and secure it. Apply terminal grease, then connect the positive cable first, followed by the negative. Ensure everything is tight and stable.
