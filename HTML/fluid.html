Step by step guide on : How to change your brake fluid




Drain the old fluid

Remove the brake master cylinder, take off the top, and remove as much fluid as you can. Taking the car up on axle supports and removing all four wheels is the best method to handle this.


Change brake fluid

Add fresh brake fluid to the master cylinder until it reaches the FULL line.


Flush the calipers/wheel cylinders

To remove the old brake fluid, release the bleed nozzle located at the back of each brake pedal or wheel cylinder.


Bleed the brakes

Check the master cylinder's braking fluid level and make sure it is topped off.


Repeat the process

Work your way through all of the callipers and wheel cylinders by bleeding each one in turn.
Whenever you or someone else replaces your braking fluid, dispose of the used oil in an eco-friendly way. Before discarding wasted fluid, you should review the regulations that apply to ASR (traction control), ESP, SBC, and cars with hybrid or electric propulsion.
