Step by step guide on : How to change your



1. Gather Tools & Materials

Gather a jack, jack stands, lug wrench, g-clamp or caliper tool, and a basic toolkit. Some calipers require Torx or Allen bits, so check beforehand. You may also need brake fluid. Always consult your owner's manual for the correct type.

2. Remove the Tire

Loosen the lug nuts before jacking up the car. Raise the vehicle, secure it with a jack stand, and remove the tire. The brake assembly should now be visible.

3. Remove Slider Bolts & Lift Caliper

Remove the bolts holding the caliper. Carefully lift it off without disconnecting wires. Inspect the brake pads—if worn down to about 1/8 inch, they need replacing.

4. Remove Old Pads & Clips

Take out the old brake pads and hardware. Clean and inspect if reusing. Take a photo beforehand to remember placement.

5. Compress the Pistons

Push the pistons back into the caliper using a g-clamp or piston tool. Apply steady pressure and be patient.

6. Check Brake Fluid

As pistons retract, brake fluid levels may rise. Monitor the reservoir and remove excess fluid if needed.

7. Install New Brake Pads

Apply brake lubricant where needed and install the new pads into the caliper, avoiding contact with friction surfaces.

8. Reattach Caliper & Wheel

Place the caliper back, tighten the bolts, and reinstall the wheel. Repeat the process on the other side.
