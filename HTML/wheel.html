How to Replace Your 0riginal Sterring Wheel With an Aftermarket Purchase



1. Unplug the battery

Prioritize safety! Disconnect your car's battery and give it at least five minutes before acting. If your automobile has an airbag, this step makes sure it won't unintentionally deploy when you remove the original steering wheel. It also reduces the possibility that wires will come into contact and result in a short circuit.

2. Take off the steering wheel cover

Remove the steering wheel cover, which contains the horn and airbag if one is installed. After removing the cover, carefully disconnect the wiring that connects the horn and airbag, if any. Place the horn pad and/or airbag in a secure location after handling them carefully.

3. Install the Hub Adapter


Feed the horn wire through the centre and mount your new hub adapter. Keep in mind that hub adapters are car-specific, so ensure you’ve purchased the correct one for your vehicle. Secure the hub adapter in place, following the manufacturer’s instructions.

4. Attach the New Steering Wheel

Place the spacer on top of the hub adapter, then fit your new steering wheel using the screws provided. Connect the horn wire to the appropriate terminal and secure the wheel in place. Finally, reconnect the battery and test the horn to make sure everything works as expected.
